About Jean Louis Hardy

AI & Automation Strategist for Service Business Owners

Service businesses are the backbone of the economy. HVAC companies, plumbing operations, electrical contractors, landscaping firms, cleaning services - these businesses employ millions of people and generate billions in revenue. The owners who built them spent decades mastering their craft, building teams, and serving their communities.

Now the market is shifting. AI and automation are not replacing these businesses - they are making the best ones dramatically more valuable. The gap between a "good" service business and a "great" one is increasingly defined by technology adoption. And that gap shows up directly in exit multiples.

Why I Write About This

I started The Automation Edge because I saw a disconnect. On one side, there is an explosion of AI tools purpose-built for service businesses - from intelligent scheduling platforms to AI-powered financial analysis. On the other side, most service business owners over 50 are either unaware these tools exist or unsure how to evaluate them.

That gap is a problem. Not because these owners are behind - they have built incredible businesses without any of this technology. But because buyers are already pricing AI readiness into their offers. Private equity firms, strategic acquirers, and sophisticated individual buyers are looking at operational data and asking: "How much of this runs on manual processes that AI could optimize?"

If the answer is "most of it," that is either a discount on your valuation (because the buyer has to invest in modernization) or it is a premium (because you already did the work and can prove the results).

I write to help owners capture that premium.

My Approach

Real data. Real companies. Real math.

Every article on The Automation Edge is grounded in actual market developments. When I reference Revenium's AI Outcomes platform, ServiceTitan's AI scheduling features, or Xero's partnership with Anthropic, it is because these are real products shipping to real businesses today. When I model the financial impact of AI deployment, the numbers come from industry benchmarks and published case studies.

I do not write about AI theory. I write about AI that is generating measurable returns for service businesses right now - and what that means for your company's value.

Who This Is For

The Automation Edge is for service business owners who:

  • Have built a business generating $5M to $50M in annual revenue
  • Are thinking about an exit within the next 1 to 5 years
  • Want to understand how AI affects their business valuation
  • Are willing to invest in technology that creates documentable ROI
  • Prefer data-driven analysis over hype

You have built something valuable. My goal is to help you make it worth more - and prove that value to buyers with hard numbers.
